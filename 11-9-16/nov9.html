Lightcloud Release Notes: November 14, 2016

Today’s release incorporates a host of useful new features:

  • fault detection that notifies you when a lamp fails
  • site scenes to let you create scenes using zones across multiple areas
  • new scheduling options make it easy to set up recurring events and let you incorporate zones from multiple areas into your schedule
  • Mandarin and Spanish language options
  • behavior schedules let a Lightcloud device do di erent things depending on the time of day
  • a new 15-minute power report, required by many energy incentive programs
  • device counts and filters to keep you organized
  • a new place to find Lightcloud release notes

New Scheduling Options

With this release, scheduling has moved to its own section:

In the Schedules view, you can now schedule events involving Zones across multiple Areas.

Setting up schedules works just like before, except now you have the ability to specify a date or date range when the schedule should be active, as well specify that the event repeat on a daily, weekly, monthly or yearly basis.

Selecting a language is easy. Just click on the settings icon, then click one of the links at the bottom of the page.

Fault Detection

Lightcloud can now tell you when a lamp burns out!

With the new Fault Detection feature, Lightcloud monitors a Zone’s baseline power consumption, then notifies you via email if consumption drops significantly below the baseline to give you added safety and security.

To enable Fault Detection for a Controller or Ceiling Sensor, first go to the Devices View.

Then, choose the Controller or Ceiling Sensor, click Edit for that Zone, and turn on Fault Detection.

Note:

  • Power monitoring must be enabled for any Zone where you want to use Fault Detection.
  • Once enabled, Fault Detection takes about three days to monitor baseline usage before it is able to send alerts.

To specify email addresses that should receive alerts, click on Fault Notifications in Settings.

15-minute Power Report

Many energy incentive programs now require power consumption data recorded every 15 minutes.

Lightcloud makes meeting this requirement easy. Customized 15-minute reports for your Site are available by calling 844.LIGHTCLOUD or emailing support@lightcloud.com.

Device Counts and Filters

The Devices view has been refreshed to show the quantity of each type of device you have installed. Plus, you can click on a device type to filter the list.

Release Notes

Last but not least, these and all future release notes will be available in the Lightcloud web app at Settings—>Release Notes. (URL is https://control.lightcloud.com/#/settings/releaseNote).
